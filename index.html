About Me

Hey! I'm an easily-nerdsniped student who loves watching anime and going on spontaneous adventures. I also like blogging about conviction, cryptography, and movie/travel recs. I also deeply care about open education for MIT and Harvard, and give my money away as grants for cool open source work.

I work on open source zero knowledge cryptography right now, because I think pseudonymity is a critical unlock for human coordination. In the past, I worked across fields like multi-tissue aging research, equivariant molecular neural networks, applied machine learning, and healthcare.

Projects

Here are the best ones. Check them out!

  • ZK Email: Anonymous Proofs about Email Data

    Co-invented (and still co-steward) ZK Email (prove.email), which lets you prove identity via emails you have received, whistleblow securely, or send money via sending emails.

  • Proteus: Perceptual-Hash based Image Provenance for AI

    Demo'd a cryptographic framework (proteus.photos) to find if an image was generated by an AI, institution, or person. It is robust to transformations, while keeping generated and queried images private.

  • PLUME: ZK Voting on Ethereum, via Determinisic ECDSA Signatures

    New nullifier scheme for pseudonymity on Ethereum, via ZK-SNARKs of a new signing scheme for ECDSA keys. Cryptographically proved security in Master's thesis. Implementation here and published as ERC-7524.

  • Auto-Latex Equations add-on for Google Docs

    Coded, branded, marketed the add-on for real time LaTeX mathematical expression rendering. Over 30M users. 4.5+ star rating. More info at autolatex.com. Worked with team to bring to Google Slides.

    Run a group house with my friends Raffi and Marley in Cambridge, MA. Recently moved to SF!

  • Ideas Microgrants Program
